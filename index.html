Peakly — Full Product

Peakly

Solo designer, 0-1 full product

Peakly is a food discovery app — think Tinder for meals. Swipe through dishes, save what you're craving, share the recipe with whoever's cooking, and let the app build your weekly menu and grocery list automatically.

I joined as solo designer on a 0→1 team, after UX foundations were set. Peakly isn't a painkiller — it's a lifestyle app. That means design is the entire reason someone opens it, keeps using it, and tells a friend. I built it to be brag-worthy.

PROJECT (ii)

DemandPay — reimagining payments

Demand Pay

2 Designers, UX, Visuals, 3D

DemandPay is a subscription billing and payment recovery platform — think of how a cable TV company needs to remind thousands of customers to pay, renew, or change their plan. DemandPay automates that entire flow, sending payment reminders and direct payment links via WhatsApp, SMS, and email so businesses never have to chase customers manually.

I owned the full product — UX, visual design, and 3D across both the B2B and B2C sides. This was the project where I pushed my craft the furthest: designing for two audiences at once, while making a fintech product feel premium enough to trust and simple enough to act on.

PROJECT (iii)

Mercle — Redefining WEB 3

Mercle

2 Designers, UX, UI, Visuals, 3D

Mercle is a Web3 identity platform solving one of the internet's oldest problems — you never really know if someone online is a real person. Think of it as "Login with Google" but every verified Mercle ID is guaranteed to be a real human, giving platforms and communities a trust layer that the internet has never had.

I worked across UX, visual design, mascot design, gamification, email visuals, and the app — essentially the entire visual language of the product from scratch. The challenge was building an identity strong enough to carry a Web3 product into mainstream trust.
